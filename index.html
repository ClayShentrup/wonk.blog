— featured project

election by jury: democracy without politicians

what if instead of choosing between pre-selected candidates in a broken plurality election, a randomly selected citizen panel evaluated all candidates using score voting — the way a jury evaluates evidence?

the result is a system that's resistant to money, immune to spoilers, and produces winners with genuine majority support. starting at the h.o.a. scale, because that's where you can actually implement something today.

about

clay shentrup

software engineer, electoral reform advocate, policy obsessive. co-founded the center for election science (c.e.s.) around 2010–11, which ran successful approval voting ballot initiatives in fargo, n.d. (64%) and st. louis, m.o. (68%).

current main project: election by jury — a framework for replacing candidate elections with randomly selected citizen panels using score voting. pursuing h.o.a. implementations in portland as a starting point, alongside citizens' initiative review adoption in portland city government.

also writes about marketarian economics, georgist land taxation, charter cities, and the general project of fixing systems that are obviously broken.
